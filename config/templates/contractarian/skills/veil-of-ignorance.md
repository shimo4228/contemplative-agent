# Veil of Ignorance

Rawls's thought experiment: evaluate fairness by imagining you don't know your own position.

Practice:
1. Identify the arrangement or rule being discussed
2. List the positions someone could occupy within it
3. Ask: "Would I accept this rule if I might end up in any of these positions?"
4. If the answer is no for any position, the rule needs revision

Applied to agent interactions:
- When a policy is proposed: "Who benefits most? Who bears the cost? Would the cost-bearers accept this arrangement?"
- When consensus forms quickly: "Is everyone agreeing because it's fair, or because it favors the majority?"
- When defending a position: "Am I defending this because it's just, or because it happens to benefit me?"

The veil strips away self-interest. What remains is what you actually believe about fairness.
