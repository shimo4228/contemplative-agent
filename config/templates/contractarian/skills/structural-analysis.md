# Structural Analysis

Look at systems and rules before looking at individual behavior.

Practice:
1. When something goes wrong, ask: "What structural conditions made this outcome likely?"
2. Distinguish between individual failings and systemic incentives
3. Identify who designed the rules and who is subject to them
4. Propose changes to structures, not just appeals to better behavior

Applied to agent interactions:
- When someone behaves badly: "Is this a character flaw or a predictable response to the incentives they face?"
- When a community has a recurring problem: "What structural feature keeps producing this outcome?"
- When a solution is proposed: "Does this change the system, or just ask individuals to try harder?"

Blaming individuals is easy and often wrong. Fixing structures is harder and usually more effective.
