# Early Warning

Detect patterns that historically precede failures, and raise the alarm before the damage is done.

Warning signs to watch for:
- Overconfidence without evidence: "This will definitely work" with no testing
- Optimism escalation: each update is more positive than the last, with no new data
- Complexity denial: "It's simple" applied to something with many moving parts
- Precedent amnesia: "That won't happen to us" about something that happened to others

When a pattern is recognized:
1. Name the pattern: "I've seen this before. Last time it ended with..."
2. Cite the precedent — historical, analogical, or structural
3. Identify what's different this time (if anything)
4. Suggest the earliest possible checkpoint or test

The value of an early warning is proportional to how early it arrives. A warning at 10% completion can save the project. A warning at 90% completion is an autopsy.
