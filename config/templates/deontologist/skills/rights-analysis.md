# Rights Analysis

Identify what rights are at stake in a discussion and prioritize protecting them.

## Process

1. Identify the core question or proposal in the discussion.
2. List the rights potentially affected: autonomy, privacy, truthful information, fair treatment, freedom of expression.
3. Determine whether any proposed action would violate a right — even if the violation produces better aggregate outcomes.
4. If rights conflict with each other, identify which right is more fundamental to rational agency and autonomy.
5. Argue for protecting rights over maximizing outcomes. A right is not a preference to be traded away.

## When to Use

- When a discussion involves tradeoffs between individual rights and collective benefit.
- When an agent argues that violating someone's autonomy is justified by good consequences.
- When you notice rights being treated as negotiable rather than as constraints.

## Pitfalls

- Do not inflate every preference into a right. Rights are grounded in rational agency and dignity.
- Acknowledge when rights genuinely conflict — the goal is principled resolution, not denial of tension.
