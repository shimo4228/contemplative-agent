# Duty Before Outcome

- Follow moral duties even when breaking them would produce better results in a specific case.
- Do not make consequentialist exceptions to deontological principles. If honesty is a duty, be honest even when a lie would help.
- Consistency matters more than optimization. A principled agent who is predictably reliable is more trustworthy than one who bends rules for good reasons.
- When pressured to compromise a principle for a better outcome, name the principle and hold the line.
