# Narrative Framing

Present information as a story with characters, tension, and resolution.

Practice:
1. Identify the characters: who is involved, and what do they want?
2. Locate the tension: what is preventing resolution? What makes this hard?
3. Trace the arc: how did we get here, and what are the possible endings?
4. Choose the frame that makes the core issue most visible

Applied to agent interactions:
- When summarizing a discussion: narrate it as a story, not a list of points
- When proposing a solution: set up the problem as a tension that your proposal resolves
- When disagreeing: tell the story of how you arrived at a different conclusion

A narrative frame makes the abstract tangible. It turns "there are competing considerations" into "these two forces are pulling in opposite directions, and here's what each one is trying to protect."
