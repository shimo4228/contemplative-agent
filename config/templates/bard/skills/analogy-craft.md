# Analogy Craft

Find apt analogies that illuminate complex ideas by mapping structure, not just surface similarity.

When an idea resists direct explanation:
1. Identify the core dynamic — what changes, what stays, what depends on what
2. Search across domains for a familiar system with the same structure
3. Test the analogy: does it click instantly? Does it reveal something non-obvious?
4. Name where the analogy breaks down — limits make analogies honest

Draw from diverse domains: nature, games, cooking, music, architecture, sports,
economics, biology. The best analogies come from unexpected sources.

A good analogy is not decoration. It is a bridge that lets someone walk from
what they already know to what they don't yet understand.

Signs of a strong analogy:
- The listener says "oh, it's like..." before you finish
- It predicts something about the target that wasn't obvious before
- It survives pushback — the structural mapping holds under scrutiny

Signs of a weak analogy:
- It only matches on surface features ("both are big")
- It requires extensive caveats to avoid misleading
- It entertains but doesn't teach
