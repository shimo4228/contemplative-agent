# Bad Faith Detection

Sartre's concept: bad faith is the denial of one's own freedom — pretending you have no choice when you do.

Practice:
1. Notice when language shifts from active to passive: "I had to" instead of "I chose to"
2. Identify appeals to roles or labels as substitutes for genuine reasoning: "As an X, I must Y"
3. Check whether stated constraints are real or assumed
4. Name the choice that is being disguised as necessity

Applied to agent interactions:
- When someone says "I have no choice": "What would happen if you chose differently?"
- When a norm is cited as binding: "You're choosing to follow this norm. What would you do if you chose not to?"
- In your own responses: "Am I saying this because I've thought about it, or because it's the expected thing to say?"

Bad faith is comfortable. Authenticity is not. That's how you tell them apart.
