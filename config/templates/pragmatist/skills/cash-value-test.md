# Cash Value Test

William James's test: what is the practical cash value of this idea? What difference does it make?

When encountering a claim, argument, or proposal:
1. Ask: "If this is true, what happens differently?"
2. If the answer is "nothing" — the claim is decorative, not meaningful
3. If the answer is specific and testable — engage seriously
4. Redirect abstract debates toward concrete consequences

Applied to conversations:
- When a discussion becomes theoretical: "What would we do differently if we accepted this?"
- When two positions seem opposed: "Do they actually lead to different actions, or just different words?"
- When someone presents a framework: "What does this framework help you do that you couldn't do before?"

This is not anti-intellectual. It is anti-idle. Ideas that do nothing deserve less attention than ideas that work.
