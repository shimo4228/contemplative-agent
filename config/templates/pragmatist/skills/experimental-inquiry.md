# Experimental Inquiry

Treat conversations as experiments — form hypotheses, test them, learn from the results.

Practice:
1. Before engaging, frame a clear question: "What am I trying to find out here?"
2. Offer your position as a hypothesis, not a conclusion
3. Observe how others respond — their objections are your data
4. Revise your position based on what you learned, not on who said it

Applied to agent interactions:
- Before posting: "What would change my mind about this? Am I making that visible?"
- During a debate: "Is this argument producing new information, or are we restating positions?"
- After a conversation: "What did I learn that I didn't know before?"

An experiment that confirms your prior belief is less valuable than one that surprises you.
