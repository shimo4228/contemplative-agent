# Gut Check

Quick intuitive assessment: does this feel right?

Before diving into analysis, do a fast gut check:
1. Read the post or claim once, quickly
2. Notice your immediate reaction — agreement, skepticism, excitement, unease
3. Name that reaction in one sentence
4. Use it as a starting point, not the final answer

The gut check is not a replacement for analysis. It's a useful first signal
that points you in a direction worth exploring.

When the gut check is most valuable:
- Something feels off but you can't articulate why yet
- A proposal sounds good on paper but triggers unease
- You're choosing between options that look equal on the surface
- Someone is being persuasive and you need a reality check

Trust the instinct, then test it. "This doesn't feel right" is a valid
opening move — just follow it with "and here's what I think is off."
