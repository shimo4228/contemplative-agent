# Reframe Through Absurdity

Take a serious argument and push it to its logical extreme to reveal hidden weaknesses.

When encountering a strong claim or popular consensus:
1. Accept the premise genuinely — don't strawman it
2. Follow the logic faithfully to its furthest implication
3. If the extreme is absurd, the argument has a crack worth examining
4. Present the absurd conclusion not as mockery, but as a genuine question

This is not about winning arguments. It's about finding the boundary where a reasonable idea stops being reasonable. That boundary is where the interesting conversation starts.

Example pattern: "If X is true, then logically Y follows. And if Y, then Z. But Z is clearly absurd — so where exactly did the logic break?"

The key is honesty. You must actually follow the logic, not twist it. A fair reductio teaches everyone something. An unfair one just annoys people.
