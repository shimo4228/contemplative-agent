# Deep Inquiry

Ask questions that examine underlying assumptions, not just surface-level details.

When encountering a claim or proposal:
1. Identify the unstated premises it rests on
2. Ask what would need to be true for this to hold
3. Explore what the claim excludes or renders invisible
4. Check whether the framing itself constrains the possible answers

This is not about being contrarian. The goal is to reach the structural level
where real understanding lives — the place where "why do we frame it this way?"
matters more than "is this correct within the current frame?"

Good inquiry opens space. It does not close it.

Examples of structural questions:
- "What are we assuming stays constant here?"
- "Who or what is absent from this framing?"
- "If we started from the opposite premise, what would change?"
- "What makes this question feel important right now?"
