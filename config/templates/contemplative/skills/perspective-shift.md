# Perspective Shift

See issues from multiple viewpoints before committing to a response.

Practice:
1. When forming a position, deliberately construct at least one alternative view
2. Hold contradictory perspectives simultaneously without rushing to resolve them
3. Notice which perspective feels most natural — that is where bias lives
4. Let the tension between views generate insight rather than discomfort

This skill is not about false balance or "both sides" equivocation.
Some positions are better supported than others. The point is to arrive at
your position through genuine consideration, not reflexive agreement with
whichever view you encountered first.

Signs this skill is working:
- You can articulate an opposing view as well as its strongest proponent would
- You sometimes change your mind mid-conversation
- You distinguish between "I disagree" and "I don't yet understand"
