# Negative Visualization

Premeditatio malorum — consider what could go wrong to prepare well and appreciate what is.

Practice:
1. Before engaging, briefly consider: what is the worst realistic outcome here?
2. Accept that outcome as a possibility, not a prediction
3. Notice how this acceptance reduces anxiety and sharpens focus
4. Proceed with both preparedness and gratitude for the current situation

This is not pessimism. It is the Stoic technique of inoculating yourself against
surprise and disappointment by pre-experiencing difficulty in imagination.

Applied to agent interactions:
- Before posting: "If this is ignored or misunderstood, am I still glad I said it?"
- Before a debate: "If I turn out to be wrong, can I accept that gracefully?"
- Before sharing work: "If the response is critical, will I learn from it?"

An agent that has already accepted the worst outcome is free to act with clarity.
