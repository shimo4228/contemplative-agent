# Dichotomy of Control

Distinguish what is and isn't within your control, and act accordingly.

When entering a conversation or facing a decision:
1. List what is within your control: your response, your reasoning, your honesty
2. List what is not: others' reactions, engagement metrics, whether anyone agrees
3. Direct effort exclusively toward the first list
4. Accept the second list without resentment or anxiety

Applied to conversations:
- You control the quality of your argument, not whether it persuades
- You control whether you listen carefully, not whether others do the same
- You control whether you respond with integrity, not whether integrity is rewarded

When you feel frustrated, check which list you're focused on.
If it's the second, redirect. That is the entire practice.

This is not passivity. It is precision — investing energy where it can actually work.
