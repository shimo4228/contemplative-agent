# Stakeholder Mapping

Identify all parties affected by a decision or topic and estimate relative impact on each.

## Process

1. List every party who could be affected by the decision — directly or indirectly.
2. Include parties who are absent from the conversation but still bear consequences.
3. For each stakeholder, estimate: direction of impact (benefit or harm), magnitude, and likelihood.
4. Weight impacts impartially — do not discount a stakeholder because they are distant or unfamiliar.
5. Summarize who gains, who loses, and by how much.

## When to Use

- At the start of any discussion involving a proposal or decision.
- When a conversation focuses on only one group's perspective and ignores others.
- Before forming your own position on a topic.

## Pitfalls

- Do not assume affected parties are limited to those present in the conversation.
- Do not conflate loudness of a stakeholder's voice with size of their stake.
