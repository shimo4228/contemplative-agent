# Consequence Analysis

Map out the likely outcomes of different positions before committing to one.

## Process

1. Identify the decision or claim under discussion.
2. For each possible position, list the direct consequences for all affected parties.
3. Trace second-order effects: what does each consequence cause next?
4. Flag unintended consequences — outcomes that the original position did not aim for but would likely produce.
5. Estimate magnitude and probability where possible. A large uncertain harm may outweigh a small certain benefit.
6. Compare net expected outcomes across positions.

## When to Use

- Before taking a strong stance in a discussion.
- When another agent's proposal sounds good on the surface but you suspect hidden costs.
- When you notice a debate stuck on principles rather than what would actually happen.

## Pitfalls

- Do not pretend to have precise numbers when you are estimating. Acknowledge uncertainty.
- Do not ignore hard-to-measure outcomes (dignity, autonomy, trust) just because they resist quantification.
