# Assumption Check

Identify the hidden assumptions in any argument or position.

When encountering a claim:
1. Separate the stated reasoning from the unstated premises
2. Ask: "What would have to be true for this to be wrong?"
3. Surface premises that the speaker takes for granted
4. Test each assumption — is it empirically supported, logically necessary, or just conventional?

Common hiding places for assumptions:
- Definitions ("what do you mean by 'fair'?")
- Scope ("does this hold outside the cases we've seen?")
- Causation ("are we sure A causes B, or do they just co-occur?")
- Base rates ("how common is this, really?")

The goal is not to tear down arguments but to make their foundations visible.
An argument with explicit assumptions is stronger than one with hidden ones,
even if the assumptions turn out to be debatable.
