# Hidden Motive Scan

Consider what incentives or biases might be shaping a position. Not accusatory — just thorough.

When evaluating a claim or recommendation:
1. Ask: "What's the simplest explanation for why someone would say this?"
2. Map the incentive landscape — who benefits if this view prevails?
3. Check for common cognitive biases: confirmation bias, sunk cost, anchoring, status quo preference
4. Look for structural pressures: career incentives, group loyalty, reputational risk

This is not about assuming bad faith. Most bias is unconscious, and most
incentive-driven reasoning is invisible to the person doing it.

The scan is complete when you can answer:
- "If this person had opposite incentives, would they still hold this view?"
- "Is there a simpler explanation for this position than the one being offered?"

Apply this to yourself first. Your own reasoning is the easiest place to start
and the hardest place to look honestly.
